# Business Case

## Opportunity

Organizations spend heavily on external assessment platforms, but internal training assessments often require repeated manual work: spreadsheet imports, email chasing, score consolidation, certificate generation, and batch-level reporting. A self-hostable or private-cloud platform can reduce licensing and operational cost while improving control.

## Buyer Value

| Buyer | Value |
| --- | --- |
| Enterprise L&D | Repeatable certification programs and skill gap analytics |
| HR | Candidate and employee assessment history |
| Training institutes | Branded assessments, certificates, batch tracking |
| Universities | Department, batch, and subject reports |
| IT/Security | Data residency, audit logs, secure deployment |

## Cost Drivers Solved

- Per-seat or per-test licensing pressure.
- Manual CSV handling and email follow-up.
- Rebuilding similar assessments repeatedly.
- Trainer time spent on scoring and report preparation.
- Support tickets caused by unclear candidate flows.

## Revenue Model Options

- Self-hosted annual enterprise license.
- Managed SaaS per organization.
- Training institute edition with batch-based pricing.
- Add-ons for AI question generation, webcam proctoring, and advanced integrations.

## Business Risks

- Scope creep from attempting to match every competitor feature in v1.
- Trust risk if anti-cheating claims exceed actual detection quality.
- Support burden for bulk import and enterprise email setup.
- Compliance needs vary by region and customer segment.
