# Market Research

## Research Summary

Public product pages and customer-facing materials show that platforms such as HackerRank, Codility, Mercer Mettl, TestGorilla, HackerEarth, CodeSignal, LeetCode Enterprise, and iMocha generally focus on hiring, technical screening, proctoring, skills libraries, and assessment delivery. Internal training teams need many of those capabilities, but with stronger emphasis on reusable internal content, organization governance, batch reports, trainer workflows, branding, certificates, and integrations.

## Common Pain Points

| Pain Point | Impact | SkillForge Response |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Expensive licensing | Internal training programs become costly at scale | Self-hostable and organization-based pricing model |
| Question management complexity | Trainers duplicate, lose, or overuse questions | Versioning, approval workflow, usage analytics |
| Limited customization | Tests feel hiring-oriented rather than internal | Branded templates, custom sections, custom certificates |
| Weak trainer dashboard | Trainers cannot easily monitor live cohorts | Live candidate dashboard with status and alerts |
| Email management gaps | Manual invitations and reminders | SMTP integration and template-driven campaigns |
| Candidate management friction | CSV lists and repeat attempts are hard to manage | Candidate profiles, history, batch enrollment |
| Randomization limits | Question leakage and repeated tests | Pools, difficulty distribution, weights, per-candidate randomization |
| Reporting friction | Manual score reporting wastes trainer time | Candidate, batch, department, organization, subject reports |
| Internal training mismatch | Hiring-first products under-serve certification | Training-centered workflows and certificates |
| Security concerns | Leaked links or shared credentials | Magic links, OTP, encrypted tokens, audit logs |
| Anti-cheating pressure | Remote assessments need credibility | Fullscreen, tab, paste, device, IP, and suspicious activity scoring |
| Integration problems | HRMS/LMS systems stay disconnected | REST APIs and webhooks |

## Market Position

SkillForge Enterprise should position itself as an internal assessment operating system rather than a hiring-only screening tool. The primary differentiator is operational depth for trainers and L&D teams: templates, versioned content governance, automated communication, certificates, learner history, and analytics.

## Source Notes

- HackerRank, Codility, CodeSignal, HackerEarth, and LeetCode Enterprise represent technical hiring and coding assessment patterns.
- Mercer Mettl, TestGorilla, and iMocha represent broader skills assessment, proctoring, and enterprise assessment workflows.
- OWASP and NIST guidance inform security baseline assumptions.
